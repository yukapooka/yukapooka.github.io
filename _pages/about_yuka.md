I started in computational biology and bioinformatics, working at the intersection of data, systems, and healthcare. Over time, I became increasingly drawn not just to technical analysis itself, but to the broader decisions and workflows around it — how complex outputs are interpreted, acted on, and integrated into real-world settings.

Across healthcare and biotech environments, much of my work evolved toward product-shaped and systems-level problems. I was most engaged when the challenge involved clarifying complexity, improving how work moved across people and systems, and helping technical efforts become more usable, actionable, and operationally meaningful. Although my titles were not formally PM or TPM titles, many of the problems I worked on required the same underlying skills: problem framing, technical translation, stakeholder alignment, prioritization, and structured execution.

As I worked more closely with predictive models, analytics, and decision-support contexts, I became increasingly interested in a deeper question: not just whether a system performs well technically, but how it shapes human judgment. In high-stakes environments, model performance alone is not enough. Trust calibration, workflow design, uncertainty, and institutional context all influence whether a system actually improves decisions. That interest led me to deepen my focus in Responsible AI and behavioral decision science, especially around designing human-AI systems that support decision quality rather than simply optimize model outputs.

Today, I’m most drawn to work at the intersection of healthcare product, decision systems, governance, and execution. My background gives me a strong foundation in technical and scientific environments, but the work I want to keep doing is broader: building systems that are not only technically sound, but also usable, trusted, and well integrated into real workflows. I’m particularly interested in healthcare PM, TPM, product strategy, Responsible AI, and decision science or behavioral science roles where technical depth, cross-functional clarity, and long-term systems thinking matter.
